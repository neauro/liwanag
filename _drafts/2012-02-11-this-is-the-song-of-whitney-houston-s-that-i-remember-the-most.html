--- layout: post title: "This is the song of Whitney Houston's that I remember the most:" date: 2012-02-11 comments: false ---




This video used to be played before every movie showing on the military base near where I lived. Everyone always had to stand for this part, pushing aside thick jackets prematurely blanketed on laps, balancing gallon cartons of popcorn carefully on neighboring folded-up seats. In the time that had passed since the last time I saw a movie at this theater I would forget about this video, so seeing it over and over again was always a pleasant surprise. When she sings in this video, it looks so powerful, free, and effortless.

At some point they tried playing another, more recent Super Bowl rendition of the Star-Spangled Banner, and after an outcry they resumed the Whitney video. Nowadays, the theater staff play something that I suspect was handed to them by the government: an orchestral anthem set to soldiers diving into the ocean from helicopters, saluting with nostrils flared against the setting sun.  Blatant patriotism is blatant, and somehow still not as completely amazing as Whitney Houston.
