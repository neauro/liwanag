I've been kind of annoyed with the women that are featured on Magic's creatures before; in general, male characters have a ton of diversity (with representations as Rakish Heir, Thraben Doomsayer, Disciple of Griselbrand), whereas women tend to be like...Crossway Vampire, or even Morkrut Banshee, with only half a face to speak of but wearing a corset which features her perky boobs.

Imagery like this says: in Magic World, men can be a variety of things, they can be young or old or fat or wrinkled, but if women are there, she better be supple and breastful, like, maybe something that a guy can look at while his opponent is thinking out their turn.

Part of the reason why I started getting into Magic during the Innistrad block is because I loved the women werewolves and the fact they could become enormous unsexualized female creatures.  The release of Avacyn Restored, the final set of Innistrad, came with a ton of other fun female(-assigned?) creatures, like...


Farbog Explorer (source)

Farbog Explorer: Look at you.  Look at your hat.  You are awesome.


Sigarda, Host of Herons (source)
Sigarda, Host of Herons: who shows more variety to angelic armor than what's shown on other angels like Herald of War the Angel token, who have that odd cleavage-bearing armor that most fantasy characters have.  In general, her femininity is also less pronounced than other Angel creatures.

Bruna, Light of Alabaster (source)
Speaking of angels, there's Bruna, Light of Alabaster, who (despite potentially flashing everyone that she flies over), is a darker-skinned angel, which is pretty neat.

Cathedral Sanctifier (source)
Speaking of darker-skinned characters, Cathedral Sanctifier is also pretty neat -- though her skin still strikes me as pretty light, which I mention still due to the privilege that lighter-skinned PoC have over darker-skinned PoC, as the movie Dark Girls points out in clarity.  Other cool priest-y creatures: Holy Justiciar, Devout Chaplain.

Thraben Valiant (source)
Thraben Valiant: Awesome armor, awesome impossibly-curved whip-thing, awesome hat.

Riders of Gavony (source)
Riders of Gavony: It's a female human knight!  Go go go!

Kruin Striker (source)
Kruin Striker: I guess she wants to kill the wolfir, which is a little sad for me story-wise, since I like them...but I also appreciate that this provides more cool "women who have agendas!" flavor.  She also helped me in most of my pre-release matches, so, totally legit.

Elgaud Shieldmate (source)
Elgaud Shieldmate: So cool.  I only regret being unable to play her with Latch Seeker during pre-release.

Gryff Vanguard (source)
Gryff Vanguard: Another sweet human knight, plus she's riding a cool heron-gryphon thing, so.

Champion of Lambholt (source)
Champion of Lambholt: Again, cool for adding to the variety of looks/things in Innistrad, and also for potentially overcoming the multitude of spirit tokens in the metagame, haha.


Lingering "Oh Well :/" Things:

  • The majority of creatures in Innistrad are still white.  I mean, I guess the entire set is inspired from European fantasies, and traditional Europe is white-skinned, but I just find it mind-boggling that it's easier to imagine angels and demons living in a fictional world than it is to imagine darker-skinned humans.  It's also frustrating to continually see high fantasy scenarios the realm of mostly-white characters.  Would the world-building really suffer for having more PoC there?  Would people get up in arms about it?  If so, consider that players would be arguing that they want their fictional worlds to be mostly white, and then realize what this means for semi-subconscious racism.
  • Even though there are more women and more types of women filling different roles in Innistrad, I would consider them all still conventionally beautiful in their thinness and youth.  Contrast, again, with the male characters: Diregraf Escort, Silverblade Paladin, and Timberland Guide (all of whom I consider middle-aged-looking), Nightshade Peddler (not conventionally beautiful, period), Alchemist's Apprentice (which just makes me realize that I haven't yet noticed a close-up of a female character that just shows her face, you know, minus boobs or identifying skirt), Nephalia Smuggler (who is clearly not thin)...and so on.
  • Both of the wolfir cards (Wolfir Silverheart and Wolfir Avenger) look pretty masculine to me, though CML thinks they look pretty androgynous.  The wolfir is what happened when Avacyn repressed the werewolf curse in certain humans, so given the density of female werewolves, I was a little disappointed there weren't more female-looking wolfir...but in my mind perhaps they chose to stay as werewolves and live far away and free :)
  • The card Triumph of Ferocity, which somehow got printed despite depicting sexualized violence.  Like, really?  Aren't these cards vetted?  Why did a variety of designers/developers/artists think it was okay to depict a man pinning a woman by her throat, raising a blade above her, with his knee between her thighs?  A full post about this here.
