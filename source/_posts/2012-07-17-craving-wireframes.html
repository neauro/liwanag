The past few days I've been trying my hand at making wireframes, which are basically very simple website layouts. I was like, Cool, this is cool, I've already got tons of very simple website layouts!

a wild wireframes appears!

I scanned in some old notebook pages for my old "Craving" project, which would be a website that would aggregate photos of foods at restaurants, and enticingly display them at random to hungry people wondering where to eat. I then cleaned them up and was imminently bewildered at how to best place together these sketches.

maybe like...this...?

Then someone was like, "You should try Illustrator," so I finally installed my copy of it and found these great tutorial videos and set off. I have to admit that Illustrator-made wireframes in general bewilder me -- it seems so much easier to just make sketches, and then move on to code. But, Illustrator is pretty fun to use.

illustrator: the perfect square-drawing software
