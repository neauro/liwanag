I've started another story.  I'm confident about this one.  I didn't finish last year's NaNo but I'm placing my palms on it, shoving and shoving until its sputum can be resurrected as a passable plot.

Mmm.

I've discovered (not unexpectedly) that writing on the computer is, for me, a lost cause.

i'm going to run out of moleskine soon

There are too many distractions.  I've been weaned too well to the Information Age.  A newly-transferred Twitter packet is the sweetest nectar.  The "(1)" notification that appears to the left of the title of my Facebook tab sends my endorphins flying (though 90% of the time it's just a request from CastleVille).

I've also started assembling a playlist of songs Pandora has suggested to me, which I've found surprisingly stirring of the misery-hope emotions that fuel my inspiration so well.  You can find it here.





Last but not least, an excerpt which I hope won't jinx my endeavors:

"It will feel better once you open your eyes," Candle suggested, sounding shameful now, "sorry I can't keep it smooth," but though Win indeed felt sick, she couldn't bring herself to do it -- to open her eyes, to look down and realize that she was miles away from home. If she fell, how long would it take for her to return? To fall past Quartzal and Agatava and all of Tenor Anthem, the journey that had taken days in elevators, in short trains weaving on zippered tracks past caves of stationary monsters, in carriages fixed to the back of cliffdragons whose gangly shoulderblades made the entire carriage sway as they navigated aggressive granite striae. The journey of days could be undone in hours of freefall. Minutes, if she didn't hit anything.
