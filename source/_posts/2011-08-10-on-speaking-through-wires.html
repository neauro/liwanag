I had an interview today! It was for a position that I felt was made for me -- involving writing, drawing, design, some management too. (Managing soothes me because I feel like I have an eye on everything that's going on.) Despite my enthusiasm I think I still wasn't able to explain myself properly, and I blame it in some part on Skype. I struggle with communicating effectively over electronic things like phones and video-chats. People's eyes don't meet, there are no visual cues, they don't see that I'm fiddling with the cork of this delicious Cupcake Red Velvet wine that I finished yesterday as I think.

The internet connection is faulty. Did they understand what I said? Did I really understand what they said? Am I being too silly? Are my feelings of pure love for video game design and story reaching you through these pixels?

Man, I love the comfort of words that you can think through before you write, then think through after you write, then revise if necessary.

One thing they asked was for further demonstration of my ability to sketch things out in perspective, or make layouts, so after RPG today (every Tuesday with the other interns) I drew out a short rendition of Little Red Riding Hood, the whole of which you can view on Tumblr, where it looks much prettier, thanks to the whole tiled photosets thing.

I don't think I've drawn things "with perspective" since the endless corridors of elementary school, but these were fun to do. :)
