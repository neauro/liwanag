Those seedlings we planted are finally sprouting!

Unfortunately, other than the swiss chard (which has such a distinctive red stem), I'm not sure what the others are, since we forgot to label them.

i think it's cilantro on the left, and carrot on the right? maybe spinach?

I also prepared for transplanting the huckleberry plant into the ground by first checking out some online resources and the acidity of the pot that it was in with a pH tester.


sweet acid bro

Since the pot soil was pretty acidic, I tried to amend some of the garden soil with peat moss to keep it that way. Blueberry plant also got some peat moss to keep it healthy, since the pH tester registered a perfect neutral for the soil that it was in.

I also discovered a lime tree on sale at the grocery store, and placed it to live near the indoor herbs currently hanging out on the dryer. The herbs are a thai basil and a stevia, both gifted to me from a "free" bin at the farmer's market. I'm not really sure what I'll do with either of them yet, particularly since I think the thai basil is about to flower and die soon.

indoor homies

The stevia is getting pretty tall so I may try and cut it back as suggested, though my inner novice worries that an entire plant might just go belly up after being nipped.
