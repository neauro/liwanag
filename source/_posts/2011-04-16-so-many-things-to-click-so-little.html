So a couple of days ago I attended the launch of Zynga's Seattle office. It was pretty delicious; I guess Zynga has their own pomegranate martini with Pop Rocks in it. They sank to the bottom though and become saturated with t3h alcohols pretty quickly. My other favorite food was this sort of fried wonton with the most melty sashimi in it, which came with a sort of guacamole-with-edamame substance. Also present: hand-made marshmallows dipped in chocolate and covered in nuts and thinly sliced almonds.

I went with David and Fransiskus and we all managed to get pictures with Mark Pincus XD

and then he went off to mingle elsewhere

Then we chatted with some of the other engineers at the event, who all seemed to be peripheral to games in some way -- I even saw the guy who gave the original concept for one of my research projects. Mostly I was pretty hyped to get Zynga-world money, which we did end up getting at the very end in a gift bag, along with a nifty thermos.

swag, yeeee

Then, because David and Fransiskus like it so much, I started playing CityVille. Which has actually turned out to be really fun o___o I've already been playing FarmVille for a while, and I started FrontierVille because of hearing the Civilization designer created it, but CityVille strikes a perfect balance in between them. I like the clean artwork and the little people that wave at you and the general friendliness of the whole thing. The sound effects for when you pick up different resources are also really happy-inducing somehow. For seriose. They're on par with Zelda SFX for uncovering mystery doors and opening treasure chests.

so many things to click and feel productive with <3

Though, I guess a Zynga game is also much more fun definitively if you have a ton of people to play it with, unlike in FrontierVille, where I desperately keep posting requests for Juicy Berries and Critter Milk and have been getting no response. There have been shy and sick baby creatures on my homestead for months.

none of you are ever getting help.  ever

I definitely want to incorporate the "popping out of things" like coins and XP in Get Across too, maybe so that when you move onto a tile or something you get rewarded with little coins and bits. I'm not sure if that's too much of a "casual" mechanic that will end up turning people off, though...I guess for now we'll put it off into an advanced feature.
