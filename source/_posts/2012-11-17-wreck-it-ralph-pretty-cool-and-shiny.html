Went against the Skyfall rush to see Wreck-It Ralph the other weekend. The popularity of Skyfall didn't mean that Wreck-It Ralph had open seats, however, so Andou and I opted to see the still-open 3D movie.

[spoilers]

It was super beautiful, and all the video game references at the beginning were pretty delightful, yet not, in my opinion, cheesy and overwhelming. It was fun to hear people practically scream at the Metal Gear Solid reference, though my personal favorite was a shot of Sonic blinking and losing his rings after being hit by that spaceship thing :D

Overall I was really impressed by the clarity of the story, especially given the risk of confusion resulting from relying on too much video game esoterica or fanservice. Certain words and certain settings threw their weight into the plot's goings-on, and it was nice to feel that the things you saw were, for the most part, not just shiny fluff, but would actually deliver on some interesting and relevant happening.

One thing that I just couldn't stand was the character Vanellope. I couldn't muster up sympathy for her at all. @__@ I guess the idea is that her immaturity, selfishness, and spontaneity are okay because she's a child and she has enormous caramel apple-colored eyes? As a character who seems solely there to be "adorable" and act as a motivation for the protagonist, I felt mixed...and had a lot of weird feelings about the character interactions in general.

On one hand, it's nice that Ralph finally finds "goodness" not in the accumulation of shiny extrinsic things (like that "Hero" medal), but rather in helping others (specifically, Vanellope). On the other hand, the whole "I'm Ralph and just seeing Vanellope be happy is what I need to be happy :')" had a heteronormative tinge -- Helping children be happy is the most important thing in the world! -- and ultimately I feel like I wish Ralph had come to learn more about what he does personally that make him uniquely happy, rather than having his newfound happiness hinge so much on helping a specific person realize their aspirations. I mean, the movie is called Wreck-It Ralph, right?

The inevitable marriage between the only other female main character and the only other male main character was also hugely wince-inducing, just because it seemed so...forced...and inevitable. (Ack! I wince just thinking about it!)

Anyway, Vanellope seems unexpectedly popular on Tumblr, so I'm still musing on the reasons why I don't like her. Maybe I missed something...
